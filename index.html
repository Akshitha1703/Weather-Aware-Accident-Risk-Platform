Project Overview

What this platform delivers

The Weather-Aware Accident Risk Platform combines historical accident data and weather conditions to answer: When and where are drivers at higher risk, and under which weather patterns?

The platform is built as a Power BI report with six pages: Home, Overview, Weather Impact, Location Risk, Trends, and a dedicated How to Use page. Each page is designed for a different decision-making need from high-level trends to localized drill-downs.

  • Summarizes nationwide accident volume and severity under different weather conditions.
  • Highlights patterns across states, cities, and time of day/week/year.
  • Supports data-driven awareness for road safety campaigns and planning.

Dashboard Page · Weather Impact

How weather conditions influence accidents

The Weather Impact page focuses on understanding which weather conditions, visibility levels, and precipitation patterns contribute to higher accident frequency and severity.

Key insights from the Weather Impact dashboard

  • Most accidents occur in “Clear” or “Fair” weather, highlighting the role of driver behaviour.
  • Reduced visibility (< 5 miles) and moderate to heavy precipitation increase accident likelihood.
  • Certain weather categories (fog, mist, snow) are associated with a higher share of severe accidents.

Dashboard Page · Location Risk

Which states and cities show higher risk?

The Location Risk page helps users compare accident risk across states and cities, supporting prioritization of interventions and awareness campaigns.

Key insights from the Location Risk dashboard

  • States such as Texas, Florida, and California have the highest total number of accidents.
  • Smaller states, including some with challenging climates, show a higher proportion of weather-related severity.
  • City-level breakdowns highlight specific hotspots where accidents consistently cluster.

When do accidents most frequently occur?

The Trends page reveals temporal behaviour by year, month, weekday, and time of day, helping identify high-risk periods under different weather patterns.

Key insights from the Trends dashboard

  • Fridays and evening rush hours show higher concentration of incidents.

Data & Integration

Data sources and integration workflow

The platform is built on public datasets and a reproducible pipeline using Power Query and Power BI modeling.

Primary data sources

  • US Accidents Dataset (Kaggle) – accident-level details including timestamp, location, severity, and weather snapshot.
  • Weather Dataset (Kaggle) – hourly environmental measures such as temperature, visibility, humidity, wind speed, and precipitation.

Data is hosted in SharePoint/OneDrive and connected to Power BI using web connectors for dynamic refresh.

Cleaning & integration steps

  • Removed duplicates, standardized timestamps, and split fields into Date, Time, Hour, Month and Weekday.
  • Separated the original file into Accident_Data and Weather_Data logical tables.
  • Handled null values using Fill Up/Down and profiled columns to validate data quality in Power Query.
  • Created keys and relationships to join accident events with corresponding weather snapshots.
  • Designed a star-schema model and used DAX to define KPIs and metrics used across all dashboards.

Directions

How to use this Power BI report

The How to Use page in the report provides in-report directions; the summary below is for stakeholders and new users accessing the platform for the first time.

Report navigation & interaction

  • Use the page tabs at the bottom (Home, Overview, Weather Impact, Location Risk, Trends, How to Use) to move between dashboards.
  • Apply slicers at the top of each page to filter by state, city, year, weather condition, and time-related fields.
  • Click on bars, lines, or map regions to cross-filter the other visuals and focus on a particular subset.
  • Hover over points and bars to view detailed tooltips, including counts and severity percentages.
  • Use the clear/reset buttons on slicers (or the built-in eraser icons) to return to the full dataset view.

Interpreting the insights

  • Executive/Home pages – high-level KPIs for overall communication with leadership.
  • Weather Impact – used by safety teams to understand which conditions require extra alerts.
  • Location Risk – helps planning teams prioritize high-risk states and cities.
  • Trends – supports scheduling campaigns and interventions around peak risk periods.
