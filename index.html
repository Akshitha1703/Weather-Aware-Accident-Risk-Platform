Dashboard Pages & Insights

Each dashboard page was designed to answer a specific analytical question and includes clearly defined KPIs, visuals, and insights derived from the Power BI report.

1. Overview Page

Purpose: Provide a nationwide snapshot of accident trends and key performance indicators.

  • KPIs:
    • Total Accidents
    • Severe Accidents
    • Weather-Related %
    • Average Severity
  • Core Visuals:
    • US Map – Accident Density by State
    • Top 5 Weather Conditions Causing Accidents
    • Severe vs Total Accidents (Pie Chart)
  • Insights:
    • Over 1.04 million total accidents were recorded nationwide.
    • Approximately 65% of these occurred during Clear or Fair weather, indicating that traffic volume and driver behavior outweigh purely weather conditions.
    • California, Texas, and Florida account for the highest total accident counts across all years.

2. Weather Impact Page

Purpose: Understand how specific weather conditions influence accident likelihood and severity.

  • KPIs:
    • Average Temperature (°F): 64.8
    • Average Visibility (mi): 9.2
    • Average Precipitation (in): 0.05
    • Average Humidity (%): 65.9
    • Average Wind Speed (mph): 7.6
  • Core Visuals:
    • Total vs Severe Accidents by Weather Condition
    • Average Temperature, Precipitation, and Visibility by Condition
    • Weather Impact Narrative (text insight)
  • Insights:
    • Most accidents occur under Fair and Clear conditions (good weather), due to overconfidence and high traffic volume.
    • Visibility below 5 miles and precipitation above 0.3 in significantly increase accident severity.
    • States like Vermont, Washington, and Wyoming show >70% weather-related accident ratios.
    • Extreme weather (Fog, Dust Storms) has fewer cases but far higher severity ratings.

3. Location Risk Page

Purpose: Identify which states and cities experience the highest accident density and severity.

  • KPIs:
    • Total Accidents
    • States Covered: 49
    • Average Severity Index
  • Core Visuals:
    • Filled Map – Accident Density by State
    • Top 10 States by Total Accidents
    • City-Level Accident Table (City, State, Total, Severity, Avg Temp)
  • Insights:
    • California, Texas, and Florida lead in total accident volume.
    • Urban areas like Los Angeles, Dallas, and Miami consistently appear as top accident-prone cities.
    • Some states like Wyoming and Vermont show lower total accidents but higher average severity, implying more serious outcomes per crash.

4. Time-Based Trends Page

Purpose: Discover when accidents are most frequent — by year, month, weekday, and hour.

  • KPIs:
    • Total Accidents
    • Average Accidents per Month
    • Peak Hour Accidents
  • Core Visuals:
    • Monthly Accident Trend (Bar Chart)
    • Accidents by Weekday (Column Chart)
    • Hourly Trend (Line Chart)
  • Insights:
    • December and January record the highest accident counts — correlating with winter and holiday travel.
    • Fridays show the most accidents across the week (end-of-week rush + fatigue).
    • Peak accident hours: 7–9 AM and 4–6 PM (commute periods).
    • Year-over-year analysis shows slight decrease after 2021, suggesting effective post-pandemic road management.
