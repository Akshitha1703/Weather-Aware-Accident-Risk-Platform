Executive Overview

Overview dashboard – high-level risk picture

The Overview page is designed for executives and non-technical stakeholders. It provides a quick, yet complete, picture of accident risk across the United States. Users can slice the analysis by State, City, Year and Weather Condition using the filters at the top.

Key KPIs

  • Total Accidents – count of all recorded accidents in the selected period.
  • Severe Accidents – total number of accidents involving higher severity levels.
  • Weather-Related % – share of accidents that occurred under non-clear weather conditions.

Core visuals

  • Accident Density by State (Map) – shows which states carry the heaviest accident burden.
  • Top 5 Weather Conditions Causing Accidents – bar chart comparing conditions such as Fair, Clear, and Mostly Cloudy.
  • Severe vs Total Accidents (Pie) – quick view of how serious the event distribution is.

Key findings from the Overview page

  • Accidents are not limited to bad weather – Fair and Clear conditions still account for a large share of total incidents, highlighting the role of traffic volume and driver behaviour.
  • A significant proportion of accidents are still weather-influenced, especially under cloudy and overcast conditions.
  • Accident hotspots are concentrated in a handful of states, making targeted safety programs feasible.
Weather Impact Analysis

Weather Impact dashboard – how conditions change risk

The Weather Impact Analysis page explores how different weather parameters influence both the likelihood and severity of accidents. A KPI row summarises the typical environment during accidents:

Main visuals

  • Top 5 Severe Weather Conditions – side-by-side bars for Total vs Severe Accidents by condition (e.g. Fair, Clear, Mostly Cloudy).
  • Top 5 Conditions by Average Temperature – bar chart showing extreme hot-weather categories such as Blowing Sand and Dust Storms.
  • State-wise Weather-Related Accident Counts – table listing states with high percentages of weather-related incidents.
  • Key Insights text – narrative that explains what the numbers mean in plain language.

Key insights from Weather Impact

1. “Good” weather still carries risk. Fair and Clear conditions together account for the highest number of accidents. These are high-traffic periods where drivers tend to travel faster and may be less cautious.

2. Cloudy conditions increase severity. Mostly Cloudy and Overcast weather show a noticeable share of severe accidents, suggesting that reduced lighting and intermittent rain/fog contribute to more serious outcomes.

3. Extreme heat conditions are rare but risky. Weather types such as Blowing Sand, Dust Storm and Funnel Cloud have the highest average temperatures and are associated with more challenging driving conditions, even if they occur less frequently.

4. Some states have a very high weather-related accident ratio. States like WY, VT, DC, RI and WA show weather-related percentages above 70%, meaning the majority of their severe accidents occur under non-clear conditions.

Location Risk

Location Risk dashboard – where accidents concentrate

The Location Risk page answers the question “Where are roads most dangerous?”. It combines a national map with state-wise bars and a city-level table to let users move from a macro to micro view.

KPIs

  • Total Accidents – all records in the current filter context.
  • Average Severity Index – average severity level across those accidents.
  • States Covered – number of states with at least one recorded accident.

Main visuals

  • Accident Density by State (Map) – bubble map showing where accidents cluster geographically.
  • State-wise Accident Count (Bar) – ranks states by total accidents.
  • City-Level Details (Table) – top cities with accident counts, severity totals and average temperature, allowing deeper investigation.

Key insights from Location Risk

  • Accident density is highest in a small set of states, making them clear targets for focused interventions and infrastructure improvements.
  • Urban and suburban cities contribute a large share of total accidents, reflecting higher vehicle volume and intersection complexity.
  • Some states show a relatively lower accident volume but a higher severity index, indicating that when accidents do happen there, they are more serious.
  • City-level views highlight specific localities that frequently appear near the top, signalling where targeted awareness campaigns or enforcement might have the greatest impact.
Time-Based Trends

Time-Based Trends dashboard – when risk is highest

The Time-Based Trends page focuses on when accidents are most likely to happen. Slicers allow filtering by Year, Month, Weekday, Hour and State.

KPIs

  • Total Accidents – for the selected time period.
  • Average Accidents per Month – used to benchmark whether a month is above or below normal.

Core visuals

  • Month-Wise Accidents – stacked columns for Total vs Severe Accidents by month.
  • Accidents by Weekday – compares weekdays to identify the highest-risk days.
  • Accident Trend Over Years (Line) – shows how total and severe accidents evolve over time.
  • Smart narrative text – highlights which months/years stand out most.

Key insights from Time-Based Trends

  • Certain months show significantly higher accident counts than the annual average, often aligning with winter weather or holiday travel periods.
  • Weekdays such as Wednesday and Friday tend to show higher accident volumes in the sample, suggesting an effect of mid-week fatigue and end-of-week travel.
  • The year-over-year line chart indicates a gradual change in total accidents and severe accidents, helping stakeholders understand whether risk is trending upward or downward.
  • Filtering by individual states reveals that peak months and peak weekdays differ across geographies, reinforcing the need for localised safety policies.
Key Insights & Recommendations

What we learned & how it can be used

Summary of key insights

  • Accidents remain high even in Fair and Clear weather, indicating that driver behaviour and traffic density are just as important as adverse weather.
  • Cloudy and overcast conditions, especially with reduced visibility, are associated with a higher share of severe accidents.
  • Several states and cities emerge as consistent hotspots, demonstrating that accident risk is geographically concentrated.
  • Time-based patterns show that certain months and weekdays carry more risk, which can be used to schedule enforcement and public awareness campaigns.
  • Weather-related accidents make up a substantial portion of total incidents in some states, highlighting a need for localized weather-aware warnings and road design improvements.

Actionable recommendations

  • Target high-volume “good weather” periods. Since many accidents occur on Clear and Fair days, authorities should promote safe-speed campaigns and distraction-free driving even under normal conditions.
  • Prepare special operating plans for low-visibility conditions. When cloud cover, fog or heavy precipitation is predicted, dynamic message signs and navigation alerts can warn drivers about reduced visibility and slippery surfaces.
  • Focus interventions on hotspot states and cities. Direct enforcement, engineering changes and local campaigns to the top-ranked locations identified in the Location Risk dashboard.
  • Align patrols and messaging with peak time windows. Use the Time-Based Trends page to schedule extra patrols and media campaigns on the riskiest weekdays and months for each state.
  • Extend the model with live feeds. Future work could connect to live weather APIs and streaming traffic data so that the dashboards move from historical analysis to near real-time risk alerts.

How non-technical users can work with this platform

The solution was deliberately designed so that a non-technical user can interact with it confidently: slicers at the top of every page allow simple filtering by Year, State, City, and Weather Condition. KPIs and narrative text explain what the visuals mean, while the GitHub documentation and this website document the full project journey – from data sourcing and SharePoint integration to Power BI modeling, dashboards and insights.
