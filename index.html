Weather-Aware Accident Risk Platform

Power BI analytics integrating real-time weather and traffic data to understand accident patterns across U.S. regions.

Overview Data Source Pipeline Preparation Modeling Dashboards Budget Documentation

Project Overview

The Weather-Aware Accident Risk Platform leverages open-source datasets and live connections to analyze how environmental factors influence accident frequency and severity across U.S. highways. Hosted dynamically on SharePoint, it updates automatically through Power BI’s web data connection.

Data Source

Primary dataset: US Accidents (Updated) — Kaggle. It combines multi-year accident records with weather data from NOAA and U.S. DOT sources.

Included Data

  • Accident_ID, Severity, Start_Time, End_Time, Distance, State, City, Zipcode
  • Temperature, Humidity, Visibility, Wind Speed, Precipitation, Weather Condition

Excluded Fields

Removed: Amenity, Bump, Station, Traffic_Signal, and twilight variables to streamline for weather impact analysis.

Data Pipeline

Data cleaning and pipeline creation involved downloading from Kaggle, cleaning with Power Query, splitting into Accident and Weather sheets, and connecting dynamically via SharePoint for automatic refreshes in Power BI Desktop.

Data Preparation & Profiling

Cleaning & Quality Checks

  • Removed duplicates and handled nulls with Fill-Up / Fill-Down.
  • Verified field integrity using Power Query’s column profiling tools.
  • Standardized date-time formats and geo-codes for consistency.

Derived Metrics

  • Year, Month, and Hour extracted from Start_Time.
  • GeoKey column built to connect weather and accident tables.

Data Modeling & DAX

Power BI model built using a star schema with relationships between Accident_Data, Weather_Data, Dim_Date, and Dim_Hour. DAX calculations created for total accidents, average temperature, average visibility, and custom Weather Impact Index.

Dashboard Insights

Overview Page

Summarizes national KPIs: total accidents, trend by year, severity distribution, and accident map by state.

Weather Impact Page

Analyzes the correlation of rainfall, wind speed, and visibility with accident frequency.

Temporal Trends Page

Visualizes accidents by day of week and hour, revealing peaks in rush-hour and winter months.

Regional Comparison Page

Allows state and city comparisons of severity rates relative to weather conditions.
